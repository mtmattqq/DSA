$O(\max\{f(x), g(x)\}) = O(f(x) + g(x))$

Since

$$
2 \times \max\{f(x), g(x)\} > f(x) + g(x) \geq \max\{f(x), g(x)\},
$$

and 

$$
O(2 \times \max\{f(x), g(x)\}) = O(\max\{f(x), g(x)\}),
$$

we can say that

$$
f(x) + g(x) \in O(f(x) + g(x)) \in O(\max\{f(x), g(x)\})
$$

and 

$$
\max\{f(x), g(x)\} \in O(\max\{f(x), g(x)\}) \in O(f(x) + g(x))
$$
